## The Philosophy: Math as the Engine of AI
As mentioned in my `avg-dev` project, this repository is another form of documenting my journey—this time focused on the mathematical foundations that power modern technology. My interest in mathematics began in my first year of university, but at that time, my understanding of the field was limited. I viewed AI as a "black box" or a specific script, much like ChatGPT, Gemini, or Clang, without truly grasping the complexity behind them.

I initially fell into the common trap of thinking that knowing a bit of Python and basic math would automatically make me a "Super AI Engineer." I was wrong. To truly master AI—not just to code it, but to understand it with logic—you must understand what happens under the hood.

## The Low-Level Connection
Real AI mastery requires understanding the libraries we often take for granted. Tools like **PyTorch**, **TensorFlow**, and **NumPy** are not just Python scripts; they are written in **C and C++**. These low-level languages are what allow the mathematics to communicate directly with the hardware (GPU) to perform massive computations.

My strategy is clear:
1. **Phase 1:** Build a solid career as a Software Engineer with a focus on System Performance.
2. **Phase 2:** Leverage that systems experience to bridge the gap into AI Engineering.
3. **Phase 3:** Combine Mathematics and Algorithmic optimization to build high-performance intelligence.

I am highly optimistic about this path because it focuses on deep understanding rather than memorization.

### AI

#### Understanding the Pipeline: What an AI Engineer really does

```  
     NumPy       -->      Pandas       -->   Matplotlib  -->   scikit-learn (Machine Learning)
 (create arrays     (clean & analyze)       (create graphs)         ↓
   algorithms)                                                    Deep Learning:
                                                                 - Keras/TensorFlow (option 1) (production-ready)
                                                                 - PyTorch (option 2) (research-friendly)
```

* **NumPy:** Foundations for manipulating numerical arrays and algorithms.
* **Pandas:** Used for cleaning, transforming, and analyzing structured data.
* **Matplotlib:** For visualizing data through graphs and plots to understand patterns.
* **scikit-learn:** For implementing traditional machine learning algorithms.
* **Keras / TensorFlow:** High-level and production-ready deep learning frameworks.
* **PyTorch:** Flexible and research-friendly deep learning framework.
